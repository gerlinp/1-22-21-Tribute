Jean-Jacques Dessalines

The First Emperor of Haiti

  • 1758 - Born Jean-Jacques Duclos on September 20 1758, Jean-Jacques was born into slavery on Cormier, a plantation near Grande-Riviere-du-Nord

  • 1788* - Dessalines worked on the Duclos's plantation until he was bought by a free black man named Dessalines at around the age of 30, who assigned his own surname to him. He worked for that master for about three years, until the slave uprising, but kept the Dessalines name.

  • 1791 - Along with thousands of other slaves.Jean-Jacques joined the rebellion led by Jean François Papillon and Georges Biassou. It was then he met Toussaint Bréda (later known as Toussaint Louverture)

  • 1794 - After the French declared an end to slavery, Dessalines followed Toussaint Louverture in fighting against both the Spanish and British becoming Louverture's chief lieutenant and rising to the rank of brigadier general by 1799.

  • 1801 - Dessalines gained a reputation for his "take no prisoners" for burning homes and entire villages to the ground, after commanding many successful engagements including the captures of Jacmel, Petit-Goâve, Miragoâne, and Anse-à-Veau. Dessalines even ended an insurrection in the north led by Louverture's own nephew general Moyse.

    The Rebellion was able to restore most of Saint-Domingue to France, with Louverture in control as governor-General of the colony. The French Government had gone through changes and was now led by Napoleon Bonaparte who was committed to restoring slavery in Saint-Domingue. The french dispatched an army led by General Charles Leclerc. Louverture and Dessalines fought together in defeating the army in battle for which he is most famous (Crête-à-Pierrot.)

  • 1802 - After the battle of Battle of Crête-à-Pierrot, Dessalines defected from Louverture after learning that Louverture had failed to instruct a local rebel leader to lay down his arms per the recent ceasefire agreement.In which resulted in Louverture's capture and arrest.

    When it became clear that the French intended re-establish slavery, Dessalines switched sides again to oppose the French, becoming the leader of the revolution after the capture of Toussaint.

  • 1803 - His forces achieved many victories leading up the last major battle of the revolution. The Battle of Vertières, on November 18, 1803.

    On December 4, 1803, The French Army surrendered its final remaining territories to the Dessalines Forces, which ended the only slave rebellion in world history which resulted in establishing and independent nation.

  • 1804 - On January 1st, 1804 from the city of Gonaïves. Dessalines officially declared the former colony's independence and renamed it "Ayiti" after the indigenous Taíno name, Naming himself Governor-General for life.

    .Born into slavery and having worked under white masters for 30 years, Dessalines did not trust the white French people. between February and April 1804, he had the white Haitian Minority killed by ordering the 1804 Haiti Massacre with an estimated death toll of 3,000 - 5,000 people of all ages and sexes.

  • 1805 In the city of Le Cap On May 20th, 1805 Dessalines government released the Imperial Constitution, naming Jean Jean-Jacques Dessalines emperor for life with the right to name his successor. In declaring Haiti an independent country, Dessalines also abolished slavery making Haiti the first country in the Americas to permanently abolish slavery

  • 1806 - Dessalines forced a harsh regimen of planaation labor. Dessalines demanded that all blacks work either as soldiers to defend the nation or as labourers on the plantations to raise crops and help sustain the nation. his forces were strict in enforcement, to the extent that some blacks felt as if they were again enslaved.

    Frustrated members of Dessalines administration began a conspiracy to overthrow the Emperor. Dessalines was assassinated north of the capital city, Port-au-Prince at Larnage(now known as Pont-Rouge) on October 17, 1806 on his way to fight rebels.

  • Legacy - Dessalines assasitnation did not solve the tensions within the Haitian Government, as the power vacuum led to a civil war and temporary partition of Haiti between Petion and Chrisophe.

    Shortly after his death, many men on the island changed their last names from their slave names to "Jean Jacques" in honour of Dessaline. Some historians believe these men were soldiers of Dessalines.

    For the remainder of the 19th century, Dessalines was generally reviled by generations of Haitians for his autocratic ways. However, by the beginning of the 20th century, Dessalines began to be reassessed as an icon of Haitian nationalism. The national anthem of Haiti, "La Dessalinienne", written in 1903, is named in his honour.
